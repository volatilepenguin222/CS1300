Personas and Storyboarding

Interface: Automatic Gas Stove

Since a lot of people come to my apartment to cook and people often cook with or for other people, I figured that an automatic gas stove was a reasonable public interface to highlight.

Contextual Inquiry

Sketch + Description

  • Key Interactive Components:
  • auto-ignition
  • knob labels signaling lighter component + flame strength
  • graphics assigning knobs to burners
  • knob orders attempt to align with burner order
  • front burner feature: POWER BOIL for large cookware
  • high grate height from the burner
  • open cooktop grates

This interface is meant to enable users to cook food in a more controlled way, as opposed to the more historic form of an open fire. An automatic gas stovetop, as opposed to a manual gas stovetop, allows for a simpler start by making the lighting feature of the gas emitted inherent to the stove itself (for manual, a person would need to set the gas aflame with another external device, such as a match, or lighter). This enables modern-day cooking to be both safer and more efficient.

The automatic gas stovetop interface relies heavily on knobs for control. Each knob dictates the fire-starting and heat strength for a single burner. The top of each knob has a black line indicating where the knob is currently pointing - the starting position is labeled 'Off', and moving counter-clockwise, each is labeled for the 'Light' setting, followed by the flame setting from 'High' to 'Low'. Spatially, the knobs are aligned with their respective burners in order to be as intuitive as possible for the user. Additionally, each knob is annotated with a simplified graphic of the stovetop, indicating which burner it is for by highlighting the respective burner in black. To use a burner, a user should press down on a knob and turn it to 'Light'; this results in an electric sparker setting the gas ablaze. After a few seconds to ensure lighting has occurred, the user can then turn the knob to their desired flame setting. Both front burners feature an extra-strength burner, labeled as such under their respective knobs. The high grate height relative to the burners allows for free flow of gas such that a flame can successfully light and enables a more even distribution of heat. Additionally, using grates allows users to be able to actually see of the strength of the flame, something which more visually-prone people find helpful.

Insights

Key Observations

The task given was to cook a frozen strawberry waffle using the automatic gas stove. Majority of users began by eyeing the knob interface and audibly questioning, "Now how do I do this?". Most users didn't notice the griddle burner option and opted for using a pan over the front left burner (Neither did any of them consider the 'POWER BOIL' annotation). One user did not know how to use the knobs; gas was left to emit ~20 seconds before they turned the knob somewhere close to the 'Light' position. Once a flame had lit, they left the knob in between the 'Light' position and the 'High' position, such that the the sparker continued to run, as indicated by a 'flick flick' sound that kept repeating; however, they maintained their confidence throughout the task. All users began with the flame at its highest setting, and some users lowered it when they thought the waffle was cooking unevenly. All users were very attentive to the item they were cooking and also attempted to seek external assurance that the object was done cooking.

  2. Is there anything that you like or dislike about the interface?
  3. How comfortable do you feel cooking with other people?
  4. How often do you see yourself using this type of interface?
"I really like stove, it's very efficient and intuitive. I grew up with this kind of stove - I used to use it all the time, and definitely not stopping anytime soon! Once you know the steps, it's a fairly straightforward process."
"When I first started using a gas stove I thought I had a gas leak, but since then I've learned the proper way to turn it on. I don't feel that experienced or capable in the kitchen, but I'm learning. I love the power of this stove and this cool griddle feature, didn't even know it existed until a couple weeks ago."
"I like the little infographics and the labels are clear. It's nice to be able to actually see the flame height too. The knobs seem kind of outdated if anything - it would be easier if the process of igniting the fire was like those automatic car engine start buttons."
"I actually feel way less comfortable using the stove without other people around... With my current knowledge I probably wouldn't try to use the stove alone. But when I start living alone I do really want to learn to cook, and you can do everything with a stove."
